Pluto planar orbit optimization

Calculate the optimal thrust to achieve a planar trajectory to the desired final orbit, assuming a certain specific impulse (exhaust velocity) and specific power for the engine.

The fuel structural mass fraction is 0.02. The demo delivers 1000 kg to Pluto in 3 years.

EXAMPLES: Pluto mission in 5.2 years, with 1000 kg payload and 10800 kg total dry mass, 36,700 kg fuel, with 1 MW fission reactor. This corresponds to a specific power of about 134 W/kg with a payload of 2646. Total efficiency to thrust is 15.8% and uE is 40 km/s. Total thrust is 8 N. Assumed distance to Pluto is 30.5 AU.

"Standard" mission: 1000 kg payload, 1000 W/kg specific mass, efficiency of 0.4, uE of 100 km/s

repeatPrevious = false;

nYears   = 4.5;
payload  = 1000;
uExhaust = 100; % km/s
sigma    = 740;   % W/kg
eta      = 0.3;     % Total fusion power to thrust

Calculate the optimal planar solution

Pluto's perihelion is 29.6 AU. For a launch in 2036 Pluto is closer to 33 AU distance.

d0 = PlanarHelioOptimal;
d0.rF   = AU*29.6;
d0.tF   = nYears*YEAR;
d0.mP   = payload;         % payload mass (kg)
d0.uE    = uExhaust;     % exhaust velocity, km/s
d0.f     = 0.05;         % fuel structural fraction (tanks)
% additional fields for optimization
d0.sigma = sigma;        % specific power, W/kg
d0.eta   = eta;          % thrust efficiency
d0.maxThrust = 50;       % maximum thrust (N)
d0.scale = [1;1e4;1e4];
d0.nPts = 50;

% Evaluate
if repeatPrevious
	[phi, thrust, t, data] = PlanarHelioOptimal( d0, 4000, 'iter-detailed', thrust0, phi0  );
else
  [phi, thrust, t, data] = PlanarHelioOptimal( d0 );
end
phi0 = phi;
thrust0 = thrust;

SimulatePlanarHelioTrajectory( data.mD, data.mF, thrust, ...
                   data.input.uE, data.input.r0, data.input.rF, data.input.mu, phi, t );

% Report
StraightLineReport( data );
