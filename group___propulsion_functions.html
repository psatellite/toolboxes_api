◆ ElectricThrust()

blank line function ElectricThrust ( in Isp,
in power,
in eff )

The governing equations are:

T = mdot*ue eff*P = 0.5*mdot*ue^2

so that eff*P is the jet power that is converted to kinetic energy.

Type ElectricThrust for a demo at 100 kW.

Form

[thrust,mdot] = ElectricThrust( Isp, power, eff )

Inputs

Isp (1,:) Specific impulse, sec power (1,1) Engine power, W eff (1,1) Thrust efficiency

Outputs

thrust (1,:) Engine power, W mdot (1,:) Mass flow rate, kg/s

References and(), Demo(), ElectricThrust(), and Plot2D().

◆ MassEquivalentDV()

blank line function MassEquivalentDV ( in dV,
in uE,
in mI,
in thrust )

Given a change in velocity and exhaust velocity this routine will compute the equivalent constant mass that will produce the same delta-v as a time-varying mass.

Type MassEquivalentDV for a demo. It shows that the total delta-v is the same. As expected, the time varying delta-v changes more slowly at first and faster at the end.

Units may be of any type as long as they are consistent. Examples are given.

Since version 2014.1

Form

[mEq, tBurn, mF] = MassEquivalentDV( dV, uE, mI, thrust )

Inputs

dV (1,1) Desired change in velocity (km/s) uE (1,1) Exhaust velocity (km/s) mI (1,1) Initial mass (kg) thrust (1,1) Thrust (kN)

Outputs

mEq (1,1) Equivalent constant mass tBurn (1,1) Burn duration mF (1,1) Mass of fuel

References and(), Constant(), Demo(), MassEquivalentDV(), Plot2D(), and TimeLabl().

Referenced by BilinearTangentAltitude(), and MassEquivalentDV().
